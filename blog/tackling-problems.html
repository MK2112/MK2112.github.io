Tackling Problems

February 15, 2023 - Multimodal

Like every student, I often enough faced problems that at first seemed almost impossible to solve. However, problems seeming 10x harder not nearly require 10x more effort. There's a structured approach to tackling them that I would like to share.

It's all about frameworks

When faced with a challenging problem, it's easy to become overwhelmed. It seems uncomfortable to know that you don't know. The first step towards solving any problem is to break it down into smaller, more manageable pieces. This sounds like every other dreadful motivational wallpaper, but it's yet to be disproven. The process of dissection is crucial in allowing you to gain a better understanding of the problem's topology, and to identify areas where you may be able to make progress.

Often enough people regard the hammer a more urgent priority than the nail. Even worse, the nail might turn out to be a screw, rendering the hammer ineffective at best. For example, while working on a data-related problem, one might jump towards an AI-based solution. This causes confirmation bias during the analysis of the data, a partial problem that should have been solved before concluding on what to build. Therefore, it is not only that you derive partial problems, but that you order them, interrelate them, and find weak spots in your understanding.

One after another

Once a problem is hacked into smaller parts, a reasonable next step is to do research. This may involve reading relevant literature, or consulting with experts. The goal is not to come up with a globally optimal solution, maybe not even a solution at all, but a deeper understanding of the problem and a set of potential solutions or approaches that may be worth considering. Make sure you ask. Maybe a question of yours opens a new perspective. This research gives you a roadmap by which the partial problems can be ranked and ordered. At this stage, interrelations between them should start to become clearer.

Try, learn, try again

When you attained an understanding of the partial problems, it's time to start exploring potential solutions. This may involve prototyping, experimenting, brainstorming, or again, asking. It is important to keep an open mind during this phase, and to be willing to consider all potential solutions, even if they seem unconventional or far-fetched. The best solutions may originate from the quiestest, most volatile thoughts. As a student, you have every right to just try out what you see fit for solving your problem. That's why you are a student. You may respect the problem's environment and you may want to avoid stepping on too many toes, but experimenting never seizes to enrich experience. I view this as the most undordered, creative and liberating part of work.

Back to order?!

The final step is to implement and test the solution. This may involve writing structured, well documented code, building prototypes, or simply conducting experiments. It is now important to be as methodical and systematic as possible, as this will help to ensure that the solution is robust and effective in the long run. Ideally, you can now navigate the problem space with both theoretical and practical experience, helping you to more confidently structure and alogn your approach to the problem. Be methodical.
While this layered approach to problem-solving may seem simple, it is actually quite versatile and effective. Breaking down complex problems into smaller parts, researching the problem space, exploring solution candidates, and testing and refining a solution enabled me to tackle my most challenging problems. It is to be emphasized that the skills and techniques required for solving complex problems are not typically taught in the public education system. However, with a little bit of patience, perseverance, and a structured approach one won't deviate from, anyone can learn to approach even those problems that once seemed towering way above their head.

So if you are facing a challenge that seems impossible to solve, that has to be solved swiftly and effectively, take heart, break it down into smaller pieces. And remember that there is always a solution waiting to be discovered.
